Our Mission

Sikkim is home to over 200 monasteries, some dating back to the 17th century. These sites are not just places of worship but also repositories of priceless art, ancient manuscripts, and a rich cultural legacy. Our project aims to create a digital platform to promote tourism to these remote locations while ensuring their long-term preservation for future generations. We believe technology can bridge the gap between tradition and modernity.

Culture & Tradition

Traditions in Sikkim

Sikkimese traditions are deeply rooted in Buddhist principles, honoring both nature and spirituality.

  • **Chaam Dances:** The most vibrant tradition, these masked dances are performed by lamas during festivals. They depict morality plays and the triumph of good over evil.
  • **Lha-soel:** This tradition involves the worship of Mount Kanchenjunga as a sacred guardian deity. Festivals like Pang Lhabsol are celebrated to pay homage to the mountain and seek its blessings.

Culture of Sikkim

Sikkim's culture is a beautiful blend of its indigenous communities and neighboring regions, creating a unique and harmonious society.

  • **Ethnic Diversity:** The culture is primarily a mix of the indigenous **Lepcha** people, the Tibetan-origin **Bhutias**, and the **Nepalese**, each contributing their own language, traditions, and cuisine to the state's identity.
  • **Art and Handicrafts:** Traditional art forms include intricate Thankga painting, wood carving, and weaving. These crafts often depict Buddhist deities and scenes from religious texts.
  • **Cuisine:** Sikkimese cuisine is a blend of Nepalese and Tibetan influences. Staples like Momo, Thukpa, and Phagshapa are widely enjoyed and reflect the local ingredients and climate.
